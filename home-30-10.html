Lost potential

why alice?

Alice gets the money to the best social projects

Alice is a decentralised social impact network built on the Ethereum blockchain.

It helps social organisations (charities, NGOs, social enterprises) run projects transparently, using smart contract-based incentives to ensure their impact is independently verified and accessible to everyone.

This makes it much easier for funders (philanthropic organisations, impact investors, small donors) to identify and help scale social projects that demonstrably work, while reducing due diligence, reporting and performance management costs.

How it works

Impact investing

Alice dramatically reduces transaction costs for impact investors, thanks to cheap & reliable due diligence, automated payments and a native secondary market.

SECTION IN our whitepaper

Performance management

Alice decentralises the evaluation, monitoring and reporting of impact. This reduces administrative costs while making it easier to compare and benchmark projects.

Giving back

Part of the fees generated by the Alice platform are redistributed to social organisations as grants or revenue share to incentivise even more transparency.

Our Technology Partners
Secure & open ID

with Innovate UK & Imperial College

Mixing selective disclosure and obfuscation, Alice allows projects to collaborate by sharing information about the people they help, while respecting their privacy. Designed as an an open standard, compatible with all self-sovereign ID systems, and compliant with data protection regulations.
Impact Measurement

with the Impact Management Project

The IMP is a global, open and iterative initiative sharing consensus about how to measure and manage social impact. The principles of the IMP are integrated deep into Alice’s impact management protocols to help grant-makers, impact investors and donors to compare and benchmark social projects.
Grants DAO fund

with Charity DAO: A new way to give

Part of Alice’s revenue is channelled to a DAO-like grant-making fund to help social organisations launch high quality projects. Getting DAOs right is hard, so we’ve partnered with the experts at CharityDAO to build a bias-free, secure system.
